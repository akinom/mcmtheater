When Actors use the move method to travel on the Stage their movement is performed incrementally and takes time. The movement speed can be controlled as is explained in But no matter how fast an actor moves movement always takes at least a little time. If an Actor wants to cover a distance in literally no time it must use the jump method. Aside from the time issue jumping differs from moving in that it does not leave a trail behind.

The applet above starts a series of Actors: The actors with the hollow shapes are JumpMove actors, which alternate between jumping and moving a given assigned distance. The ones with the solid shapes are MoveDashed actors, which alternate between moving with an invisible and a visible trail. The applet assigns a move/jump distance of 5 to the blue actor, a distance of 25 to the yellow actor, a distance of 50 to the green actor, and a distance of 100 to the blue actor. Note how the MoveDashed actor move with a nice steady speed, while the JumpMove actor's speed seems irregular. They are faster though. Both modes of travel have their advantages and disadvantages.
