The mcmTheater Stage on which all Actors perform manages the progression of time. The Stage has a 'clock' which allows each actor to perform one timed step per clock tick. There are only two types of timed steps in mcmTheater:

  • movement steps
  • sleep steps
Since an actor's moves are performed incrementally in steps whose length is governed by its step size and since the Stage allows only one such step per clock tick actor's movement speed is directly correlated to their step sizes: The Bigger The Faster.

Some Actors do not move about the Stage. Instead they jump around the stage, change shape, or change color to produce interesting effects. These Actors must explicitly tell their Stage that they wish to continue execution after a given number of clock ticks. The sleep method does just that. It puts the Actor on hold for the requested number of clock ticks.
