The actor below alternates between drawing a triangle and moving to one of the triangle's corners thus slowly filling parts of the stage. The shape method finishes drawing each triangle at one of the triangle's corners. The actor randomly chooses one of the other two corners to move to so that the next triangle starts drawing at that corner. The call Dice.throwInt(2) behaves like a two sided dice returning either 0 or 1. The actor moves 20 pixels forward if the dice returned 0, thus reaching the triangle's next corner. In the other case it turns 60 degrees to the right before moving 20 pixels which transports the actor to the other corner.

Since the actor turns 60 degrees every second round (on average) it should randomly 'circle' around the center of the stage where it started. On this web page the stage is quite small and the actor spends a considerable amount of time stuck at edge. The second actor below solves that problem.

The actor below adds an if statement to the above actor's code. It checks after moving to a corner of the last triangle drawn whether its new position is still inside its stage. If it is not inside (! means not in Java) it moves 20 pixels backwards returning to its previous position inside the stage.
