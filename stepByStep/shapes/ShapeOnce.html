The actor in the applet below moves along the edges of a triangle with an edge length of 40 pixels. It moves 40 pixels between turns and makes a total of three turns whose angles add up to 360 degree. Thus the actor points back to its original direction when it is done.

This second actor draws the same triangle as the one above but does so forever. Note that after drawing one triangle the actor ends at the same position pointed in the same direction as before calling its triangle drawing shape method. Thus calling its shape repeatedly causes the actor to travel the same triangular path again and again and again ...

The actor's program is a modification of the first actor. The new shape method contains the same statements as the previous actor's act method. This actor's act method simply calls the triangle drawing shape method within its while (true) loop, which is Java speak for "Do it Forever."
