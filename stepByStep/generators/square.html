The two generator actors on this page are based on a square drawing actor. They shoot a actor off whenever they reach the corner of their squares.

This initial actor is based on the square drawing actor above. It defines a helper method shoot() that creates a OnceAcross actor, sets its direction and position to be the same as its own direction and position and starts the created actor on the stage. The initial actor forever 'shoots' a OnceAcross actor off, moves along the side of its square, turns 90 degrees, shoots a new actor, moves along the next side, turns 90 degrees, .....

This initial actor adds a few statements for beautification purposes. The first two statements in its act() method create and assign a big blue ellipse to the initial actor. The third statements sets the initial actor's trail with to 3 pixels. The shoot contains two additional statements, one sets the new actors shape to a circle shape, the other sets the new actor's trail width to be the same as the initial actor's.

Note that the initial actor and the actor it shoots off move together along the square's side to the next corner. The initial actor turns while the OnceAcross actor keeps moving forward. This did not become obvious in the previous version since both actors had the same shape so that the OnceAcross actor's shape completely covered the initial actor's shape. The initial actor's shape is drawn below because the mcmTheater framework draws actors in the order that they started on the stage. Thus older actors, that is the ones that are on stage longer, are drawn under younger actors.
